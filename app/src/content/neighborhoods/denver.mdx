Denver is the lake's west-shore option and the one most newcomers overlook
because it's across the water from I-77. If you ask somebody in Cornelius
or Davidson about Denver, you'll sometimes hear it called "too rural" or
"too far" — both of those are partially true and partially someone
defending their side of the bridge.

The honest version: Denver has more land, quieter roads, a real west-shore
sunset, and a weekday rhythm that feels meaningfully slower. The
trade-offs are retail density and commute. If you want to walk to dinner,
don't move to Denver.
